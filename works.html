I am fine.

“How are you?”, “How’s it going?”, “How have you been?” These are a few of the typical greetings you might receive every single day from your friends, family and even some random people in the elevator on your way to work. However, when is the last time you answered “I am great!” from the bottom of your heart?

People nowadays are very good at hiding their feelings since the distance between one another is greater due to the indifferent world. People might greet you by asking “how are you” but it’s more like a “standard format” of greeting than a message with emotion and connection. In addition, the society is a stressful existence that triggers us to cover and disguise our mental state. Positive people with a smile on their faces are always more flavored by the public. That is, we hide our negative feelings behind the smile to fit in the society.

Inspired by John Yuyi, a Taiwanese-born New York-based artist who is famous for her temporary facial tattoo art, I want my artwork to reflect the real life struggle we encounter every day. The colorful smiley face stickers are the positive side we force ourselves to show people; however, when we look through the emptiness of the fake expression, we see the true concealed feelings behind the positivity.
